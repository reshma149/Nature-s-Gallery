Onam Pookkalam is a vibrant floral decoration made to celebrate Onam,
a harvest festival in Kerala.

Ona Sadhya is a grand vegetarian feast served on banana leaves during the Onam festival in Kerala. It consists of a variety of dishes, including rice, sambar, avial, thoran, and payasam, showcasing Kerala’s rich culinary tradition. The meal emphasizes harmony and togetherness, as it is enjoyed by family and friends sitting together. Ona Sadhya symbolizes prosperity, gratitude, and the spirit of sharing during the festive season.

More about Kerala

Kerala, known as "God's Own Country," is a state in southern India renowned for its scenic landscapes, including backwaters, beaches, and lush greenery. The culture of Kerala is a blend of Hindu, Christian, and Muslim traditions, reflected in its festivals, art forms, and rituals. Traditional dance forms like Kathakali and Mohiniyattam, along with martial arts like Kalaripayattu, are integral to its heritage. Festivals such as Onam, Vishu, Eid, and Christmas are celebrated with great enthusiasm, promoting unity and cultural harmony. Kerala’s cuisine, known for its use of coconut, spices, and seafood, complements its rich cultural diversity. The state also places great importance on education, literature, and Ayurveda, making it a unique blend of tradition and modernity.Kerala’s architecture reflects its connection with nature, with traditional homes called “nalukettus” built to harmonize with the environment. Temples, mosques, and churches in the state are known for their distinctive designs and intricate woodwork.
